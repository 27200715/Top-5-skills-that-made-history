The Maradona Turn is an “on the run” move, so start by dribbling.
Let’s say the player wants to turn to the right. The player will
put their right foot on top of the ball and drag the ball towards
them. The player almost stops the ball, but they stay moving. As
they slow the ball, they twirl to the left, or counterclockwise.
(They should keep their right arm up to get used to holding off a
defender.) They’ll land with the right foot in front of the ball,
which is another part of keeping it away from the opponent. Then,
they’ll use their left foot to drag the ball back into motion in
the direction they want to run. And then they’re off running to
daylight.


The firt and also the most important step is to plant your foot a
few inches from the back of the ball facing your target, then place
your dominant foot around the back of your planted leg(non dominant
foot), then curl your foot to use outside of your foot. Next, make
contact underneath the ball and finally, turn shoulders to face target
on follow through.

Are you ready for a challange? Start 3-5 steps behind the ball and try to
approach from a 35-45 degree angle. Think that you need to be about 15-20cm
away from the ball and that your toes need to point in the direction of
the intended kick. The goal is to have the least spin possible on the
ball. You want to hit it just below the center to avoid any backspin or
unnecessary height. You want your foot to strike between the instep and
the middle of the ankle joint. Avoid leaning back and keep your body
either straight or leaning slightly forward in order to further reduce
spin and to have greater control over the ball. You need to rotate your
kicking foot slightly outward and move your body in the direction of
your strike. After striking the ball, stop your leg immediately and
take no more than one or two steps to settle your body. p.solid {border-style: solid;}


Start with the ball in front of one foot, use your other foot to reach
across your body and pull the ball with the sole towards your foot’s
starting position. Then push the ball with the inside foot of your same
foot out at a 45 degree angle. You have now created a V with the ball.
Next create a V using your other foot to pull the ball back with the
sole and push the ball with the inside of your foot back the starting
position.
